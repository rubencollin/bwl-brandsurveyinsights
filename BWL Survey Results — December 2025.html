Personal Values — What Drives Us as People

About the 23plusone method

The 23plusone framework is based on decades of motivational psychology research. It maps 24 fundamental human drives — intrinsic motivations that explain why we make choices, what we find meaningful, and what brands we feel connected to.

In this survey, respondents scored each drive on importance in their own life (1–5). The bars below show the percentage who rated a drive as important or very important (4 or 5). A drive scoring above 60% is a strong cultural signal — part of what holds this community together.

Connected | Together and Idealism | Better World score at an extraordinary 97% — near-universal agreement. This community is held together by a profound belief that connection and purpose matter above all. At the bottom: status, competition, and material acquisition. This is not an ambitious or hierarchical group. It is a purposeful, warm, and freely choosing one.

The Delta — What to Amplify

The gap between now and ideal is where the most actionable intelligence lives. The most striking signal: Order | Structure jumps +24 points — the community wants BWL to become more organised and reliable. Capable | Talent (+15) and Beauty | Esthetics (+12) follow. Meanwhile, Unbound | Independent drops sharply (-18 points) — the free-spirited quality that defines BWL today is valued, but the community wants it balanced with grounded capability.

Grow: Structure & Craft
The community wants BWL to show up with more organisation, more visible competence, and more beauty. This is a maturation signal — the ambition is there; now the form needs to match it.
⚖️
Balance: Idealism → Grounded Action
Idealism drops 15 points in the ideal picture. Not a rejection of purpose — but a desire to anchor big dreams in tangible, reliable delivery. Less wandering, more purposeful movement.
🌿
Protect: Connection & Curiosity
Connection and discovery remain strong in both current and ideal. These are BWL's irreducible core — they must not be eroded in the name of structure. Bring rigour to warmth, not replace it.

BWL as a Character

Asked which story archetype fits BWL best, 58% chose Ally / Companion — standing together, providing support. BWL is not experienced as a leader, guru, or rebel. It is a fellow traveller: present, committed, in the struggle alongside others. This has a direct implication for communications: authoritative, top-down messaging will feel off. Solidarity, shared language, and "we're in this together" will land.

Voices from the Collective

Numbers tell you what people think. Words tell you what they feel. When we asked this community to speak freely — about what BWL creates, what they imagine for 2050, and what they'd put on a t-shirt — something remarkable emerged: a shared language that no one coordinated.

Words like mycelium, weaving, place, and soil appear again and again, unprompted, across 33 different people in different countries and roles. The metaphors converge. The longings rhyme. People describe not just an organisation they believe in, but a way of being in the world they want more of.

These are not polished messages. They are the raw material of a brand that is still finding its most powerful voice. Read slowly — the phrases that stop you are the ones worth keeping.
